§ 01 · Patient Hazards
Direct Physiological Risk
Patient-side hazards cluster around syringe-pump-specific failure modes: siphon, startup bolus, and dose errors from precision-critical deployments (ICU vasopressors, neonatal, PCA).
⬇️
P.01
Siphon / Gravitational Overdose
CRITICAL
Cause
Plunger disengaged from pusher + pump above patient → hydrostatic pressure (P = ρgh) drives uncontrolled flow. Also: cracked syringe, loose Luer, failed flange clip.
Harm
Lethal bolus of potent drug (opioid, vasopressor, propofol). Documented deaths in MHRA/FDA incident databases.
Magnitude
1 m height ≈ 75 mmHg — exceeds venous pressure. Full syringe can empty in minutes.
Control
Plunger-position sensor, flange clip sensor, anti-siphon valve in giving set, alarm on disengagement. Verify all at every PPM.
Standard
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.12.1.103 (bolus volume during fault)
💉
P.02
Programming / 10× Overdose
CRITICAL
Cause
Decimal-point omission (0.5 → 5 mL/hr); wrong concentration selected; unit confusion (mcg vs mg); distraction during entry.
Harm
Massive over/underdose of vasopressor, insulin, opioid, chemotherapy. Commonest reported incident class for infusion devices.
Likelihood
Without DERS: Occasional (high human error rate). With DERS hard limits: Remote.
Control
Dose Error Reduction System (DERS) with hard limits; independent double-check; standardised drug concentrations; drug library updates.
Standard
NPSA alert; NHS England DERS guidance
🎯
P.03
Startup Bolus / Mechanical Slack
MAJOR
Cause
Lead-screw backlash, pusher play, syringe plunger compression slack — all must be taken up before steady flow begins.
Harm
Delay of intended drug delivery (critical for ICU vasopressors during titration) or uncontrolled early bolus on some devices.
Magnitude
Typically 0.01–0.1 mL delay; clinically significant at low flow rates and with potent drugs.
Control
Fast-start / auto-prime feature advances pusher to contact; clinician awareness; gravimetric trumpet-curve testing at PPM captures this.
Standard
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.12.1.101 trumpet curve
🚫
P.04
Delayed Occlusion Detection
MAJOR
Cause
At low flow rates, plunger force builds slowly — occlusion alarm can take 10–60 min to trigger on 50 mL syringe at 1 mL/hr.
Harm
Drug underdose (vasopressor, sedation) causing haemodynamic instability. On clearance, stored energy releases as bolus.
Special risk
Neonatal and paediatric infusions at very low rates — alarm delay can be multiple hours.
Control
Low-threshold occlusion setting (3 levels); auto back-off to release stored energy; smaller-bore syringe (faster dP/dt).
Standard
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.12.5 occlusion alarm performance
⚡
P.05
Electrical Micro-shock (CF Part)
MAJOR
Cause
Leakage current conducted through IV fluid to intracardiac catheter / CVP line. Insulation breakdown, fluid ingress, or earth lift.
Harm
Ventricular fibrillation at currents as low as 10 µA in cardiac-vulnerable patients.
Control
CF applied part classification (≤10 µA normal, ≤50 µA single fault); IEC 60601-1 Class I earthing; annual ESR test; IEC 62353 after any mains-side repair.
Standard
IEC 60601-1 Table 1 · IEC 62353 recurrent testing
📐
P.06
Wrong Syringe Brand / Size — Dose Error
CRITICAL
Cause
Pump uses barrel cross-section (A) from user-selected brand to compute flow (Q = A·v). Wrong brand = wrong A = wrong dose.
Harm
Up to ±5% dose error — clinically dangerous for potent drugs, neonatal infusions, chemotherapy.
Example
BD vs Terumo 50 mL — different internal diameter; selecting wrong brand shifts actual delivered dose.
Control
Barrel size sensor auto-detects diameter; drug library restricts to approved brand; ward-level syringe brand standardisation policy.
Standard
Manufacturer IFU approved syringe list
🫧
P.07
Air Embolism (Limited Risk)
MODERATE
Cause
Syringe loaded with air pocket at plunger tip; tubing not primed; disconnection allowing air ingress.
Harm
Venous air embolism — though lower risk than volumetric pumps (no bag airspace, limited volume).
Likelihood
Lower than volumetric pumps. No bag-level air source; syringe must be pre-primed.
Control
Proper priming per ward protocol; some syringe pumps have optional air-in-line detector; visual inspection before start.
Standard
Local clinical priming protocol
🦠
P.08
Contamination / Infection
MAJOR
Cause
Contaminated syringe; unclean pump surface; reused single-use sets; biofilm formation; breach of aseptic technique.
Harm
Catheter-related bloodstream infection (CRBSI); sepsis — especially in immunocompromised ICU patients.
Control
Single-use syringes/giving sets; surface decontamination between patients; aseptic preparation; IPCT audits.
Standard
MHRA DB2006(06) decontamination · local IPCT policy
§ 02 · Clinical User Hazards
Use Error & Human Factors
Syringe pumps demand more cognitive load than volumetric pumps: brand selection, concentration, bolus dose, lockout interval. Human factors failures dominate the incident picture.
⌨️
U.01
Decimal / Rate Entry Error
CRITICAL
Cause
10× errors (missed decimal); rate vs. dose confusion; unit mix-ups (mcg/min vs mg/hr); time pressure; distraction.
Harm
Order-of-magnitude over/underdose — patient harm from first minutes of infusion.
Human factors
Small keypad, poor display visibility, inconsistent menu flows between pump models on same unit, shift handover gaps.
Control
DERS hard limits; confirmation screen before start; "five rights" double-check; unit standardisation; IEC 62366 usability.
🔀
U.02
Wrong Syringe Brand Selected
MAJOR
Cause
User selects wrong brand in menu; ward stocks multiple brands; visual similarity between 50 mL syringes from different makers.
Harm
Up to ±5% flow rate error — clinically significant for potent/narrow therapeutic index drugs.
Control
Ward-level single-brand policy; barrel auto-detection (where supported); drug library brand lock; training on syringe identification.
🔔
U.03
Alarm Fatigue
MAJOR
Cause
ICU patient may have 4–8 syringe pumps simultaneously; frequent end-of-syringe and near-end alarms; low-threshold occlusion alarms.
Harm
Alarm silenced without investigation; real occlusion or disengagement missed; delayed response to clinical deterioration.
Control
Three-priority alarm framework (IEC 60601-1-8); appropriate threshold setting; smart alarm management; PPM to reduce nuisance alarms.
Standard
IEC 60601-1-8 · AAMI/ECRI alarm safety guidance
🔩
U.04
Improper Syringe Loading
MAJOR
Cause
Plunger not seated in pusher grip; flange not under clip; barrel not fully in clamp; syringe forced into wrong orientation.
Harm
Siphon if pump elevated; flow inaccuracy; nuisance alarms; plunger slippage during infusion.
Control
Loading detection sensors (plunger, flange, barrel); visual/audio confirmation on successful load; staff training; pre-use checklist.
📏
U.05
Pump Height Placement Error
CRITICAL
Cause
Pump positioned high on drip stand above patient level; patient transported; stand tipped.
Harm
Hydrostatic pressure drives siphon if plunger disengages. 1 m above patient ≈ 75 mmHg driving force.
Best practice
Pump at patient heart level; anti-siphon valve in giving set; regular clinical reinforcement.
Control
Anti-siphon valve; engineering controls (plunger grip sensors); clinical protocol on pump positioning; ward-level height policy.
💊
U.06
Wrong Drug / Concentration Selected
CRITICAL
Cause
Busy workflow; similar drug names (LASA — Look-Alike-Sound-Alike); concentration variants (insulin 50 vs 100 U/mL); handover errors.
Harm
Delivery of wrong drug or wrong strength; potentially lethal for high-alert medications.
Control
DERS drug library matching the dispensed formulary; barcode scanning (where supported); independent check; ward-standard concentrations.
💉
U.07
Needlestick / Sharps Exposure
MODERATE
Cause
Line manipulation, disconnection under pressure, recapping, patient movement during line change.
Harm
Bloodborne virus transmission (HBV, HCV, HIV); occupational exposure incident.
Control
Needleless connectors; Luer-lock fittings; safe sharps policy; PPE; post-exposure protocol.
Standard
EU Sharps Directive 2010/32/EU · Health and Safety (Sharp Instruments) Regulations 2013
🔋
U.08
Battery Failure During Transport
MAJOR
Cause
Depleted battery during inter-hospital transfer or in-hospital transport; no mains backup in transit.
Harm
Infusion interruption of critical drug (vasopressor, sedation) during transport; clinical deterioration.
Control
Pre-transport battery check; ≥6 hr autonomy spec; battery low alarm (medium priority); spare charged pump available.
§ 03 · Service Engineer Hazards
Occupational & Patient-Forward Risk
The engineer carries dual responsibility: personal occupational safety and patient safety through quality of service. Post-service errors propagate forward to harm future patients.
⚠
Regulatory context Any service activity that could affect safety performance is regulated under UK MDR 2002 / EU MDR 2017/745. Post-service verification is mandatory. Failure to re-test after repair is a potential MHRA reportable event if patient harm results.
⚡
E.01
Electric Shock — Mains Voltage
CRITICAL
Cause
Live PCB fault-finding; probing energised circuits; failed isolation; damaged test leads; capacitor discharge not waited out.
Harm
Electrocution; VF; flash burns; death. 230 V AC at >100 mA lethal in under 1 second.
Control
LOTO (Lockout/Tagout); isolation transformer for live work; Category IV test leads per IEC 61010; competency training; two-person rule for live work.
Regulation
Electricity at Work Regulations 1989 · IEC 61010-1
🩸
E.02
Biological Exposure — Blood & Drug
MAJOR
Cause
Fluid ingress into pump casing (blood, saline, drug); contaminated plunger pusher; residue on syringe clamp; handling devices without decontamination certificate.
Harm
Bloodborne pathogen transmission; contact with cytotoxic/chemotherapy drug residue; skin/mucous membrane exposure.
Control
Decontamination certificate required before device reaches EBME; PPE (gloves, apron, eye protection); hand hygiene; universal precautions.
Regulation
COSHH 2002 · MHRA DB2006(06)
⬇️
E.03
Post-Service Siphon Risk (Patient Harm)
CRITICAL
Cause
Plunger sensor not functionally tested post-repair; flange clip sensor bypassed during service; alarm silenced during bench testing and not restored; anti-siphon mechanism unverified.
Harm
Patient receives lethal siphon bolus when pump returned to clinical use.
Control
Mandatory siphon test on every return to service; PPM checklist sign-off; two-stage verification for safety-critical sensors; CMMS asset record updated.
Regulation
UK MDR 2002 · MHRA reportable if patient harmed
⚖️
E.04
Force Sensor Mis-calibration
MAJOR
Cause
Strain gauge drift; incorrect reference weight used in calibration; service rig not verified; environmental temperature compensation off.
Harm
Occlusion alarm at wrong pressure — either nuisance alarms (low threshold) or delayed alarm (high threshold) → patient harm.
Control
Traceable calibration weights; OEM-specified test rig; calibration certificate retention; periodic rig verification; document serial number of rig used.
💾
E.05
Firmware / Drug Library Rollback
MAJOR
Cause
Repair replaces mainboard → old firmware; drug library overwritten during update; wrong library version loaded; incomplete update.
Harm
Outdated dose limits permit historical overdoses; missing new drug profiles force DERS bypass; alarm logic regression.
Control
Firmware & library version verification before and after service; documented in asset record; OEM-authorised update path only; functional DERS test after update.
Regulation
IEC 62304 software lifecycle · MHRA guidance
🔬
E.06
Inadequate Electrical Safety Re-test
MAJOR
Cause
PSU replaced; mains components repaired; case opened and re-closed — without IEC 62353 re-test before return.
Harm
Undetected leakage current rise or earth fault → patient micro-shock via IV path (CF classification violated).
Control
IEC 62353 tests after any mains-side repair: earth continuity ≤0.1 Ω; enclosure leakage; patient (CF) leakage ≤10 µA. Approved electrical safety analyser.
Regulation
IEC 62353 · NHS HTM 06-01
🔥
E.07
Lithium Battery Thermal Event
MAJOR
Cause
Damaged Li-ion cell; internal short; puncture during disassembly; overcharge from faulty BMS; fire at aged cells.
Harm
Fire, burns, toxic gas release (HF). Occupational injury plus facility risk.
Control
Inspect for swelling/dents before work; discharge before disassembly; fire-safe battery storage; replace per OEM cycle limits; never force a damaged cell.
Regulation
IEC 62133 · local dangerous goods policy
🧪
E.08
Chemical Exposure (Cleaning & Cytotoxic)
MINOR
Cause
Chlorine, aldehyde, IPA cleaning agents; residual cytotoxic drug on chemotherapy pumps; splash during cleaning.
Harm
Skin/eye irritation; respiratory irritation; contact dermatitis; cytotoxic exposure for oncology pumps.
Control
COSHH risk assessment; PPE; ventilation; SDS available; dedicated cytotoxic handling protocol for oncology-returned devices.
Regulation
COSHH 2002 · RIDDOR for significant events
§ 04 · Alarm System
IEC 60601-1-8 Alarm Classification
Three-tier alarm priority: high-priority (immediate risk, ≥65 dBA), medium (prompt attention), low (advisory). Independent alarm signal path ensures alarms fire even on main CPU fault.
Alarm Priority Trigger Condition Pump Response Engineer's Service Check
Occlusion (Downstream) HIGH Plunger force exceeds user-set threshold (Low/Med/High) Halt motor; auto back-off (where fitted) to reduce bolus Calibrate force sensor with reference weights; verify 3 threshold levels; test at 1 and 500 mL/hr
Plunger Disengagement HIGH Plunger position sensor loses contact / opens Halt motor; visual + audible alarm Verify switch actuation + contact bounce; clean optical path; test with known good & failed states
Flange Disengaged HIGH Flange clip sensor opens during run Halt motor; siphon-warning alarm Test clip spring force; sensor actuation; latch integrity
Wrong Syringe / Size Mismatch HIGH Barrel sensor reading incompatible with user-selected brand Prevent start; display error code Verify barrel sensor with reference syringes (multiple brands); clean optical path
Motor Stall / Step Loss HIGH Encoder pulse count ≠ commanded steps Halt motor; E-code; log event Inspect lead-screw wear, bearings, encoder disc alignment; check motor current setting
Syringe Empty / End-of-Travel MED Pusher reaches full-forward stop; end switch triggers Stop infusion; advisory alarm Test end-of-travel switch; verify pusher full-travel range
Near End of Infusion (NEI) MED Remaining volume or time < user-set threshold Audio + visual advisory; continue infusing Verify NEI threshold configuration; confirm calculation matches set volume
Battery Low MED Battery capacity <20% remaining Alert; continue on mains (if connected) Annual capacity test (discharge to 80% DoD); replace at capacity threshold
Battery Critical / Empty HIGH Battery <5% or depleted Halt pump; loud alarm before shutdown Battery health; charge circuit verification
KVO Mode Active LOW VTBI complete, KVO rate running Reduce to KVO rate (~0.1–1 mL/hr) Verify KVO rate configuration matches ward policy
Standby Timeout LOW Pump programmed but not started for X min Reminder tone Verify timeout parameter; clinical feedback on appropriateness
Mains Disconnect LOW AC mains lost; running on battery Brief audible notification; continue on battery Check AC-detect circuit; mains cord integrity
Door / Syringe Clamp Open HIGH Barrel clamp microswitch not engaged Prevent start / halt if running Clamp spring tension; microswitch actuation; anti-tamper sensor
System Fault / Internal Error HIGH Safety CPU watchdog timeout; RAM/ROM check fail Halt + persistent E-code; lock out use Read event log; reference OEM manual for E-code; return to OEM if board-level fault

§ 05 · Engineered Controls
Built-In Safety Mechanisms
Protective features mandated or recommended by IEC 60601-2-24 and ISO 14971. These are the design-embedded defences before any human response is required.
01
🔒
Mechanical · Patient
Plunger Grip with Release Pads
Claw mechanism holds plunger; disengagement requires deliberate two-point press of release pads. Prevents siphon by ensuring plunger stays gripped even when pusher stopped.
IEC 60601-2-24 · Design control per ISO 14971
02
📎
Mechanical · Patient
Flange Clip / Anti-Pullback
Sprung clip captures rear flange of syringe. Prevents syringe being withdrawn from pump during infusion — a critical siphon prevention measure.
IEC 60601-2-24 · Tested on every PPM
03
⚖️
Electronic · Patient
Pusher Force Sensor
Strain gauge on pusher measures plunger resistance directly. Three user-selectable occlusion thresholds (Low/Med/High). Primary occlusion detection mechanism — no tubing pressure sensor needed.
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.12.5
04
💊
Software · User
Dose Error Reduction Software (DERS)
Drug library with hard limits (non-bypassable) and soft limits (warning + confirmation). Prevents 10× programming errors. DERS bypass is a MHRA reportable event.
NPSA alert · NHS England DERS guidance
05
🔄
Software · Patient
Auto Back-Off on Occlusion
After occlusion alarm, pump briefly reverses to relieve stored energy in tubing compliance and plunger compression. Reduces post-occlusion bolus when line clears.
Advanced feature · verify per pump model
06
📐
Electronic · User
Barrel Size Sensor
Optical array or linear potentiometer auto-detects syringe barrel diameter. Cross-checks user-selected brand. Prevents accidental wrong-brand selection causing dose error.
IEC 60601-2-24 accuracy requirements
07
🧠
Electronic · Safety Architecture
Dual-CPU / Watchdog
Independent safety processor monitors main CPU via hardware watchdog. On main CPU fault, safety CPU halts motor through independent signal path regardless of main firmware state.
IEC 62304 Class C · IEC 61508 SIL-2
08
🔔
Software · User
Three-Priority Alarm System
IEC 60601-1-8 compliant alarm framework: High / Medium / Low with distinct audio, visual, and silence-timeout characteristics. Separate alarm signal path from main CPU for fault tolerance.
IEC 60601-1-8 · audio ≥65 dBA high-priority
09
⚡
Electrical · Patient
CF Applied Part Isolation
Patient circuit electrically isolated from mains via isolation transformer / optocouplers. Leakage current ≤10 µA normal, ≤50 µA single fault. Protects intracardiac catheter patients from micro-shock.
IEC 60601-1 Table 1 · CF classification
10
🔋
Electrical · Continuity
Battery with BMS
Li-ion battery provides ≥6 hr autonomy. BMS prevents overcharge, deep discharge, cell imbalance, and thermal runaway. Maintains infusion through power loss and transport.
IEC 60601-1 §11 · IEC 62133
11
🔐
Software · User
Key Lock / Authorisation
PIN or key-lock prevents unauthorised setting changes during infusion. Protects against accidental rate changes by patient / visitor, tampering in psychiatric settings.
IEC 62366 usability · site-specific config
12
📒
Software · Forensic
Event Log / Audit Trail
Non-volatile log of >1000 events: programmed rates, alarms, user actions, library version. Critical for incident investigation. EBME should download at PPM and after any reported incident.
IEC 62304 · MHRA incident investigation
§ 06 · Swiss Cheese Model
Defence-in-Depth
Multiple independent layers prevent hazard reaching patient. Each layer has holes — but holes must align across all layers simultaneously for harm to occur. This is how ISO 14971 and MHRA investigations frame root cause analysis.
I

Inherent Design Controls

Hazard eliminated or reduced at the engineering design stage. The lead-screw is self-locking (no free-flow on power loss). CF isolation limits leakage below arrhythmia threshold. Hard dose limits cannot be bypassed. ISO 14971 risk analysis drives all design requirements.

Self-locking lead-screw CF isolation Plunger grip mechanism Dual-CPU safety Hard dose limits
II

Automatic Protection Devices

Sensors and control logic detect hazard and halt pump without requiring human action. Force sensor triggers occlusion halt. Watchdog halts motor on CPU fault. Barrel sensor prevents wrong-syringe start. Plunger sensor halts on disengagement.

Force sensor halt Plunger sensor halt Watchdog reset Auto back-off End-of-travel switches
III

Warnings & Alarms

Alarms and advisories that require clinician to diagnose and act. Effective only if alarm is heard, correctly interpreted, and responded to promptly. Alarm fatigue is the dominant failure mode of this layer in modern ICUs.

High-priority audible Visual red flash Near-end warning Battery low alert Nurse call integration
IV

Clinical Procedures & Human Factors

Ward protocols, independent double-checks, drug library governance, and standardised concentrations reduce use error probability. IEC 62366-1 usability engineering designs the interface for safe use under realistic clinical conditions.

Five-rights check Independent double-check Standardised concentrations Pump-at-heart-level policy Shift handover protocol
V

Planned Preventive Maintenance (EBME)

Annual PPM verifies all safety mechanisms remain functional. Gravimetric accuracy, siphon test, force sensor calibration, electrical safety re-test, and alarm verification restore confidence that layers I–III remain intact. PPM is the clinical engineer's primary patient safety contribution.

Siphon test Gravimetric trumpet curve Force sensor calibration IEC 62353 ESR test Firmware & library check
VI

Post-Market Surveillance & Vigilance

MHRA Yellow Card / MIR reporting of incidents. Manufacturers are required to conduct PMS, PMCF, and issue FSCAs. Sentinel events (siphon deaths, DERS failures) trigger field safety corrective actions across the whole fleet. EU MDR 2017/745 and UK MDR 2002 set obligations.

MHRA Yellow Card Datix incident report Field Safety Notice PSUR / PMCF ECRI / AAMI alerts
