Patient Hazards
Direct physiological risk to the patient from infusion device failure or misuse
💧
Free-Flow / Gravitational Overdose
CRITICAL
Cause
Anti-syphon valve failure; door opened with tubing loaded; set removed without clamping
Harm
Rapid uncontrolled drug infusion → cardiac arrest (potassium, insulin, opioid), haemodynamic collapse
Likelihood
Low if maintained; High if free-flow test skipped at PPM
Control
Anti-syphon clamp; free-flow test on every PPM; door interlock; user training
Standard
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.11 free-flow requirement
🫧
Air Embolism
CRITICAL
Cause
Air-in-line detector failure; unprimed set; empty bag run dry; tubing disconnection
Harm
Venous air embolism → pulmonary obstruction, stroke (patent foramen ovale), death. Fatal bolus ~3–5 mL/kg in central venous line
Likelihood
Very low (protected by sensor); higher with cold IV bags (microbubbles)
Control
Ultrasonic air detector (≥50 µL threshold); empty-bag alarm; proper priming protocol
Standard
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.12.4 air-in-line detection
🚫
Occlusion / Underdose
MAJOR
Cause
Kinked tubing, clotted cannula, patient movement, positional occlusion
Harm
Drug underdose (vasopressors, insulin, sedation); rebound bolus on clearance; tissue ischaemia
Special risk
At low infusion rates (1 mL/hr), time-to-alarm can exceed 30 min — bolus stored in compliant tubing released on clearance
Control
Downstream pressure sensor + alarm; stiffer tubing reduces stored energy; appropriate occlusion threshold
Standard
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.12.5 occlusion alarm
⚡
Electrical Micro-shock (CF Applied Part)
MAJOR
Cause
Leakage current conducted via IV fluid to intracardiac catheter or CVP line; single fault condition; earth lift
Harm
Ventricular fibrillation with currents as low as 10 µA in cardiac patients
Likelihood
Very low in maintained device; higher after fluid ingress, PCB damage, or ageing insulation
Control
CF applied part classification (≤10 µA leakage); earth continuity test at PPM; IEC 60601-1 Class I construction
Standard
IEC 60601-1 Table 1 CF leakage limits
🧫
Contamination / Infection
MAJOR
Cause
Biofilm in reused tubing; contaminated IV bag; inadequate surface decontamination of pump; improper cassette handling
Harm
Catheter-related bloodstream infection (CRBSI); sepsis; endocarditis; medication contamination
Control
Single-use tubing per IFU; surface decontamination between patients; pump cleaning per manufacturer guidance
Standard
MHRA DB2006(06) decontamination; local IPCT policy
💊
Drug Library / DERS Bypass
CRITICAL
Cause
Outdated drug library; wrong drug profile selected; soft limit bypassed by user; library not loaded on device
Harm
10× overdose possible; concentration errors; incorrect dosing in paediatric/neonatal settings
Control
Dose Error Reduction Software (DERS); hard limits (unbypassable); regular library updates; pharmacist sign-off; DERS bypass events reportable to MHRA
Standard
NPSA alert; NHS England DERS guidance
Clinical User Hazards
Risks arising from use errors, alarm fatigue, and human factors in clinical settings
⌨️
Programming / Keypad Entry Error
CRITICAL
Cause
10× errors (decimal point omission); wrong concentration selected; rate vs volume confusion; distraction during entry
Harm
Massive drug overdose or underdose; most common reported infusion device incident type in MHRA/MAUDE databases
Human factors
Poor display contrast, small keypad, inconsistent menu flows across device models, time pressure on ward
Control
DERS hard limits; IEC 62366-1 usability engineering; 5 rights double-check; standardised concentrations
🔔
Alarm Fatigue
MAJOR
Cause
High nuisance alarm rate (occlusion, air, near-end); multiple pumps per patient in ICU; alarm silencing without investigation
Harm
Delayed response to genuine occlusion or air event; patient harm through missed clinical deterioration
Control
IEC 60601-1-8 alarm priority system; smart alarm management; PPM to reduce nuisance alarm rate; staff training
Standard
IEC 60601-1-8 annunciator levels; AAMI/ECRI alarm safety guidance
🪡
Tubing Misload / Wrong Set
MAJOR
Cause
Incompatible tubing brand loaded; tubing seated in wrong orientation; door closed on kinked tubing
Harm
Inaccurate flow rate (up to 20% error with wrong tubing wall thickness); occlusion alarm failure
Control
Colour-coded sets per manufacturer; cassette design prevents incorrect loading; approved set list in ward policy
Standard
Manufacturer IFU approved tubing list
💉
Needlestick / Sharps Injury
MODERATE
Cause
Manipulation of line connections; disconnection under pressure; recapping needles; patient movement during line change
Harm
Bloodborne virus transmission (HBV, HCV, HIV); occupational exposure incident; psychological impact
Control
Needleless connector systems; Luer-lock fittings; safe sharps policy; PPE; post-exposure protocol
Standard
EU Sharps Directive 2010/32/EU; Health and Safety (Sharp Instruments) Regulations 2013
🔌
Power Interruption / Battery Failure
MODERATE
Cause
Depleted battery during transfer; mains cable disconnection; unexpected power outage; battery not charged pre-use
Harm
Infusion interruption; critical drug gap (vasopressors, insulin); delayed recognition if alarm silenced
Control
Battery pre-use check; ≥4 hr autonomy requirement; battery low alarm (IEC 60601-1-8 medium priority); annual capacity test
🔄
Syringe / Bag Change Interruption
MAJOR
Cause
Dual-channel changeover gap; pump stop during bag exchange; failure to restart; delay in recognising empty
Harm
Drug gap causing haemodynamic instability (ICU); hypoglycaemia; pain return in analgesia infusions
Control
Near-end-of-infusion alarm; tandem/piggyback infusion techniques; anticipatory bag changes; drug running low protocol
Service Engineer Hazards
Occupational and patient safety risks during maintenance, repair, and testing
⚠️
Regulatory note: Any service activity that could affect device safety performance constitutes a regulated activity under UK MDR 2002 / EU MDR 2017/745. Post-service verification is mandatory. Failure to perform safety tests after repair is a potential MHRA reportable event if patient harm results.
⚡
Electric Shock — Mains Voltage
CRITICAL
Cause
Live working during PCB fault-finding; probing energised circuits; inadequate isolation; faulty test leads
Harm
Electrocution; ventricular fibrillation; flash burns; death. 230 V AC at >100 mA lethal in <1 s
Control
Lockout/tagout (LOTO); isolated transformer use during live work; Category IV test leads (IEC 61010); electrical safety training; two-person rule for live work
Regulation
Electricity at Work Regulations 1989; IEC 61010-1 test equipment safety
🩸
Biological Exposure (Blood & Body Fluids)
MAJOR
Cause
Fluid ingress in pump casing; contaminated tubing channel; aerosol from pressurised tubing; handling soiled devices without decontamination
Harm
Bloodborne pathogen transmission; skin/mucous membrane contact with infectious material
Control
Device decontamination before delivery to EBME (decontamination certificate); PPE (gloves, apron, eyewear); hand hygiene; universal precautions
Regulation
Control of Substances Hazardous to Health (COSHH) 2002; MHRA DB2006(06)
🚰
Post-Service Free-Flow (Patient Risk)
CRITICAL
Cause
Anti-syphon mechanism not tested post-repair; door interlock not reinstated; safety software not validated after firmware update
Harm
Patient receives uncontrolled bolus when pump returned to clinical use — potentially fatal
Control
Mandatory free-flow test on every return to service; complete PPM checklist sign-off; witnessed re-verification; CMMS record update
Regulation
UK MDR 2002 Regulation 21 (device performance); MHRA reportable if patient harmed
💾
Firmware Update Error
MAJOR
Cause
Incorrect firmware version; update interrupted; drug library overwritten; update applied to incompatible hardware revision
Harm
Drug library corruption (wrong dose limits); alarm logic disabled; inaccurate flow control; device unusable
Control
Manufacturer-authorised update only; version verification pre/post update; functional test including DERS after update; document firmware version in asset record
Regulation
IEC 62304 software lifecycle; MHRA guidance on software modifications
🔬
Inadequate Electrical Safety Re-test
MAJOR
Cause
Repair involving mains components; PCB replacement; power supply work not followed by formal ESR test
Harm
Undetected leakage current increase or earth fault → micro-shock to patient via IV fluid path
Control
IEC 60601-1 ESR tests after any mains-level repair: earth continuity ≤0.1 Ω; enclosure leakage ≤500 µA; patient (CF) leakage ≤10 µA; test with approved electrical safety analyser
Regulation
IEC 62353 re-test after service; NHS Estates HTM 06-01
🧪
Chemical Exposure (Cleaning Agents)
MINOR
Cause
Decontamination fluids (chlorine-based, IPA, aldehyde) used in enclosed workshop; splashing during cleaning
Harm
Skin/eye irritation; respiratory irritation from vapour; contact dermatitis with repeated exposure
Control
COSHH assessment for each chemical; PPE (nitrile gloves, eye protection); adequate ventilation; SDS/MSDS available at workstation
Regulation
COSHH 2002; RIDDOR for significant exposures
Built-In Safety Mechanisms
Engineering controls and protective features mandated by IEC 60601-2-24 and ISO 14971
01
🔒
Anti-Syphon / Free-Flow Protection
Mechanical clamp or spring-loaded valve prevents gravitational free-flow when tubing is removed or door opened. Single most important passive safety feature. Mandatory per IEC 60601-2-24.
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.11 · Tested at every PPM
02
🫧
Ultrasonic Air-in-Line Detector
Piezoelectric transceiver across tubing detects bubbles ≥50 µL. Immediately halts pump and activates high-priority alarm. Threshold set to balance sensitivity vs nuisance alarms.
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.12.4 · Threshold ≤50 µL
03
📊
Downstream Occlusion Sensing
Piezoresistive pressure sensor continuously monitors line pressure. Rising pressure triggers occlusion alarm. Threshold configurable 300–900 mmHg. Lower threshold = faster detection but more nuisance alarms.
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.12.5 · User configurable
04
💊
Dose Error Reduction Software (DERS)
Drug library with hard limits (pump refuses to start) and soft limits (warning, requires confirmation). Prevents 10× programming errors. Hard limits non-bypassable by any user. DERS bypass is a reportable event.
NPSA alert 2010 · NHS England DERS guidance · MHRA reportable
05
🚪
Door Interlock
Microswitch or Hall-effect sensor prevents pump operation with door open. Ensures tubing is correctly loaded. Also triggers clamp on door open event during infusion. Critical to free-flow prevention.
IEC 60601-2-24 · Tested at annual PPM
06
🧠
Dual-CPU Safety Architecture
Independent safety processor monitors main CPU heartbeat via watchdog. On main CPU failure, safety CPU immediately actuates mechanical clamp regardless of main software state. Compliant with IEC 62304 SIL-2.
IEC 62304 Class C · IEC 61508 SIL-2
07
🔔
IEC 60601-1-8 Alarm System
Three-priority alarm framework: High (immediate risk — audio ≥65 dBA, distinct melody), Medium (prompt attention), Low (advisory). Separate alarm signal path from main CPU ensures alarms fire even on CPU fault.
IEC 60601-1-8 · Audio ≥65 dBA at 1 m · Visual indicator
08
⚡
CF Applied Part (Cardiac Floating)
Patient circuit electrically isolated from all other conductive parts via isolation transformer or optocoupler. Patient leakage current ≤10 µA (normal) / ≤50 µA (single fault). Protects intracardiac catheter patients.
IEC 60601-1 Table 1 · CF classification
09
🔋
Battery Backup & Management
LiPo battery provides ≥4 hr autonomy. BMS prevents overcharge, deep discharge, and thermal runaway. Low battery alarm (medium priority) at 20%. Maintains infusion through power interruptions and patient transport.
IEC 60601-1 §11 · IEC 62133 battery safety
Defence-in-Depth
Swiss cheese model — multiple independent layers prevent hazard reaching patient
1
Design Controls (Inherent Safety)
Hazards eliminated or reduced at design stage. No design can produce free-flow even if all software fails (mechanical anti-syphon). Minimum possible leakage current by CF construction. ISO 14971 risk analysis drives design requirements.
Anti-syphon spring CF isolation Hard dose limits Dual CPU
2
Automatic Protection Devices (Sensors & Alarms)
Active sensors detect hazard and halt pump without requiring human action. Air detector, pressure sensor, encoder feedback, and watchdog all act automatically. Alarms alert clinical staff for secondary response.
Air detector halt Occlusion alarm Watchdog clamp Empty bag alarm
3
Warnings & Alerts (Human Response Required)
Alarms and warnings that require clinical user to diagnose and act. Effective only if alarm is heard, correctly interpreted, and responded to promptly. Alarm fatigue is the primary failure mode of this layer.
Audible alarm Visual indicator Near-end warning Battery low alert
4
Clinical Procedures & Human Factors
Ward protocols, double-checks, drug library governance, and standardised concentrations reduce use error probability. IEC 62366-1 usability engineering ensures the device interface supports safe use under realistic clinical conditions.
5-rights check Standardised conc. Independent double-check IEC 62366 usability
5
Planned Preventive Maintenance (EBME/CE)
Annual PPM verifies all safety mechanisms remain functional. Gravimetric accuracy test, free-flow test, electrical safety re-test, and alarm threshold verification restore confidence that layers 1–3 remain intact. PPM is the clinical engineer's primary patient safety contribution.
Free-flow test Gravimetric accuracy ESR test Alarm level test
6
Post-Market Surveillance & Vigilance (MHRA)
MHRA Yellow Card / MIR reporting of device incidents. Manufacturers required to conduct PMS, PMCF, issue FSCAs. Sentinel events (free-flow deaths, air embolism) trigger field safety corrective actions across entire device fleet. EU MDR 2017/745 and UK MDR 2002.
MHRA Yellow Card Datix incident report FSCA / Field Safety Notice PMS / PMCF
