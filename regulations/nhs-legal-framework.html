Section 04.1

Legislation

Primary Law
⚖️
These Acts and Statutory Instruments are the enforceable law governing NHS operations. Non-compliance carries legal consequences including fines, prosecution, and loss of registration. Updated for 2025/26 with several new regulations now in force.
1. Health, Safety & Estates
1974
Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974
The primary "Umbrella" Act. NHS must ensure health, safety, and welfare of all employees and patients "so far as is reasonably practicable".
1989
Electricity at Work Regulations 1989
Safe maintenance of the electrical grid and all plugged-in devices — from light switches to MRI machines.
2005
Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005
Mandates fire risk assessments, fire wardens, and compartmentation (fire doors) to protect non-ambulatory patients.
2002
COSHH 2002
Control of Substances Hazardous to Health — governs chemicals, lab reagents, and biohazards. Requires risk assessments, control measures, and staff training.
2013
RIDDOR 2013
Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences — legal duty to report serious accidents (e.g. needle-stick injuries) to the HSE.
1998
LOLER 1998
Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment Regulations — covers patient hoists, bed lifts, and service elevators.
1998
PUWER 1998
Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations — ensures all equipment from maintenance drills to surgical lasers is fit for intended purpose.
2. Medical Device & Clinical Governance
2002
Medical Devices Regulations 2002 (UK MDR 2002)
Foundational legislation for how devices are placed on the UK market. Classification: Class I, IIa, IIb, III based on risk. UKCA marking, registration, incident reporting, and FSCA obligations.
2021
Medicines and Medical Devices Act 2021
"Parent" Act giving government power to update regulations without new Parliament Act. Established the Patient Safety Commissioner.
2024 🆕
Post-Market Surveillance (PMS) Regulations 2024
Enforced June 2025. Manufacturers must monitor devices after NHS entry and report serious incidents within 15 days.
2017
IRMER 2017 & IRR17
Ionising Radiation (Medical Exposure) Regulations — protects patients from excessive radiation. IRR17 protects staff working with radiation.
3. Data, Commercial & Patient Rights
2018
Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR
Primary law protecting patient records and data privacy in the NHS.
2025 🆕
Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 (DUAA)
Effective February 2026. Modernizes data sharing and legally recognizes "Broad Consent" for research.
2023 🆕
Procurement Act 2023
Replaced 2015 regulations in February 2025. Focus shifts to "Most Advantageous Tender" (MAT) — value over cheapest price.
2026 🆕
WEEE Regulations 2026
Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment — safe disposal, recycling of lithium-ion batteries, and forensic data wiping of electronic medical devices.
2026 🆕
HPOA 2026
Health Professions and Occupations Act. Effective April 2026. Replaces old Health Professions Act. Introduces statutory duty for Cultural Safety and Anti-Racism.
2010
Equality Act 2010
Ensures hospitals do not discriminate and provides accessibility for those with disabilities.

Section 04.2

Standards

ISO · IEC · BS EN
Technical Engineering
IEC / BS EN
BS EN 60601-1 (2026 Edition)
"Grandfather" standard for Medical Electrical Equipment. Defines Basic Safety (no shocks) and Essential Performance.
IEC
60601-1-2 (EMC)
Electromagnetic Compatibility sub-standard. Ensures devices like MRIs don't interfere with ventilators or other critical equipment.
IEC / BS EN
BS EN 62353
Recurrent test and test after repair of ME equipment — used for annual EBME safety checks and post-repair verification.
IEC
BS EN 55011
Electromagnetic Interference (EMI) — limits radio-frequency disturbances to prevent equipment interfering with life-support systems.
IEC
IEC 62304
Medical Device Software lifecycle processes — critical for validating Software as a Medical Device (SaMD).
IEC
IEC 62366-1
Usability Engineering — ensures devices are designed using Human Factors to prevent user error in clinical environments.
Quality & Risk Management
ISO
ISO 13485:2016 (QMSR 2026 Update)
International standard for a Quality Management System (QMS) specifically for medical devices. QMSR 2026 update aligns with FDA QMSR requirements.
ISO
ISO 14971:2019
Global standard for Risk Management — teaches engineers how to weigh device failure risks against patient benefits using hazard analysis and risk matrices.
ISO
ISO 55001
Key standard for Asset Management — used by top-performing Trusts to ensure "value for money" across a device's full lifecycle.
ISO
ISO 9001:2015
General international standard for Quality Management Systems — focused on consistent service and patient/clinician satisfaction.
ISO / BS EN 🆕
BS EN ISO 15223-1 (2026 Update)
Standard for symbols used on medical device labels to ensure international recognition and safety.
Clinical & Operational
CQC
CQC Fundamental Standards
Inspection standards including Regulation 12 (Safe Care and Treatment) and Regulation 15 (Premises and Equipment).
NHS 🆕
National Standards of Healthcare Cleanliness 2025
Updated decontamination requirements. Introduces 1–5 star rating system for public transparency and Efficacy Auditing.
NHS 🆕
Protein Detection Standards (2025/26)
Shift in decontamination requiring residual protein on reusable devices to be below 5 µg per side.
NHS
GL1 Standards (Scan for Safety)
Standards for barcoding and tracking every individual device via Unique Device Identifiers (UDI) to a specific patient.
IPEM · AHCS
IPEM & AHCS Professional Standards
Standards required for technologists to be on the RCT or HCPC register. IPEM provides "Good Practice" guides for physiological and technological measurements.

Section 04.3

Frameworks

Safety · Digital · Commercial
Patient Safety & Incident Response
2026 Mandatory 🆕
PSIRF — Patient Safety Incident Response Framework
The mandatory 2026 framework replacing the old Serious Incident Framework. Moves away from blame culture toward system-wide learning. All NHS Trusts must operate under PSIRF.
2026 🆕
Martha's Rule
Fully integrated into the 2026 NHS Standard Contract. Grants patients and families the right to trigger an urgent 24/7 "Rapid Review Team" if they feel a condition is deteriorating.
Clinical
PIER Approach
Framework for managing patient deterioration: Prevention, Identification, Escalation, and Response. Showing awareness of PIER in an interview signals current clinical safety knowledge.
Legal
Duty of Candour (Regulation 20)
Legal and ethical framework requiring healthcare providers to be open and honest with patients when something goes wrong with their care.
Technical & Digital Governance
Mandatory
DTAC — Digital Technology Assessment Criteria
Mandatory for any medical technology with a "plug or a pixel". Assesses Clinical Safety (DCB0129), Data Protection, Technical Security, and Interoperability.
MHRA 🆕
AIaMD — AI as a Medical Device Framework
MHRA framework being implemented for regulating artificial intelligence in healthcare. Includes PCCPs (Predetermined Change Control Plans) for AI learning and updates.
NHS
Scan for Safety (GL1 Standards)
National framework using barcodes and Unique Device Identifiers (UDI) to track every device to every patient at point of care.
Cyber 🆕
Cyber Assessment Framework (CAF)
Used alongside DTAC. Ensures medical devices are resilient against cyber-attacks and meet requirements of the 2026 Cyber Bill.
Sustainability & Commercial
2026 🆕
Evergreen Sustainable Supplier Framework
From April 2026, all suppliers must meet Level 1 (baseline carbon reporting) to bid for NHS contracts.
2025
Procurement Act 2023 Framework
Shifts from cheapest price to "Most Advantageous Tender" (MAT) — weighing resilience, social value, and community impact.
2026
Circular Economy / "Design for Life" Roadmap
2025/26 policy framework encouraging legal reprocessing and remanufacturing of medical devices to reduce single-use waste.
Leadership & Workforce
Annual
FPPT — Fit and Proper Person Test Framework
Rigorous annual requirement for all board members and senior managers — verifies good character, professional competence, and financial soundness.
2026 🆕
Multi-Professional Regulation Framework
2026 initiative where GMC, NMC, and HCPC move toward a common framework to allow staff to work flexibly across different Integrated Care Boards (ICBs).
2026 🆕
HPOA 2026 Framework
Introduces a more independent disciplinary process and a statutory duty for Cultural Safety and Anti-Racism training for all staff.

Section 04.5

Guidance Documents

HTMs · HBNs · MHRA Docs
Estates & Technical (HTMs)
HTM 00
HTM 00 — Best Practice
Foundational guidance for healthcare engineering. Sets out general principles of healthcare engineering and service provision to ensure consistent quality across NHS technical services.
HTM 06-01
HTM 06-01 — Electrical Services
Specific guide for electrical safety in healthcare premises. Provides technical requirements for the electrical supply medical devices plug into. Mandates Uninterrupted Power Supplies (UPS) for operating theatres.
HTM 01-01
HTM 01-01 — Decontamination
Guidance for management and decontamination of reusable medical devices. Sets standards for cleaning surgical scalpels and endoscopes. 2026 shift toward "Design for Decontamination" and strict protein detection standards.
HTM 07-01
HTM 07-01 — Healthcare Waste
Classification and disposal of healthcare waste. Dictates how to dispose of devices contaminated with blood or chemicals. Links to e-Manifests for real-time digital tracking.
HBNs
HBNs — Health Building Notes
Strategic guidance on the design and planning of healthcare buildings. Ensures hospital spaces (e.g., MRI suites) are designed so medical devices can be used safely.
Clinical Safety & Alerting
MHRA Primary
Managing Medical Devices (MHRA Guidance)
Primary document for clinical engineering managers. Outlines legal responsibilities for the entire device lifecycle from Trust Board to engineer. Mandatory for CQC Regulation 15 compliance.
NHS England
National Patient Safety Alerts (NatPSAs)
Critical safety notices issued by NHS England Patient Safety Team. Issued when a specific device or process has a fatal flaw. Hospitals must act — often within 48 hours — to find and remove dangerous devices.
IPC
The "IPC Green Book"
Health and Social Care Act Code of Practice for Infection Prevention and Control. Requires all NHS bodies to have a Director of Infection Prevention and Control (DIPC).
2025 🆕
National Standards of Healthcare Cleanliness 2025
Updated standards for environmental hygiene. Introduces "Efficacy Auditing" and a 1–5 star public rating system for wards. Dictates how devices must be decontaminated before maintenance.
Sustainability & Commercial
2025
2025 Social Value Playbook
Guidance on incorporating social and environmental value into NHS contracts. Mandates that 10% of any contract score is based on Net Zero impact and local community support.
Strategy
NHS Net Zero Roadmap
Strategic document for the NHS to reach net zero direct emissions by 2040. Targets the total carbon footprint of the medical device supply chain. Drives Tray Rationalisation and similar initiatives.
2025/26 🆕
"Design for Life" Roadmap
Framework promoting the Circular Economy in healthcare. Encourages legal reprocessing and remanufacturing of single-use devices to reduce waste and move away from disposables.

Section 04.6

Policies

Trust Policies · National Mandates
Patient Safety & Clinical
National 🆕
Martha's Rule (2024/2026)
National policy in the NHS Standard Contract. Grants patients and families the right to trigger an urgent 24/7 Rapid Review Team.
2025/26 🆕
Jess's Rule
"Three Strikes" policy for primary care — a doctor must rethink a diagnosis if a patient presents three times with the same symptoms.
Legal
Duty of Candour Policy (Regulation 20)
Legal requirement to be open, honest, and apologetic with patients when care or device failure causes harm.
Mandatory
Medical Device Safety Policy
Every Trust must have a Medical Device Safety Officer (MDSO) to oversee the safety network, Yellow Card reporting, and Field Safety Notices.
Engineering & Operational
BS EN 62353
PPM Policy — Planned Preventative Maintenance
Requires all medical equipment to be maintained according to BS EN 62353 standard. Typically 95% completion target measured as a KPI.
2026 🆕
Alternative PPM (A-PPM) Policy
2026 strategy allowing engineers to use risk-profiling data to safely adjust maintenance intervals and reduce costs without compromising safety.
HTM 01-01
Decontamination Policy
Governs how reusable devices must be cleaned. Strict protein detection standards — below 5 µg per side for reusable instruments.
EPRR
Business Continuity & EPRR Policy
Manages medical devices and services during emergencies — power outages, cyber-attacks, pandemics. Must maintain UPS for critical life-support equipment.
UDI
"Scan for Safety" Policy
National programme using barcodes and UDIs to ensure every high-risk device is linked to a specific patient for full traceability.
Information Governance & Commercial
UK GDPR
Data Protection & Confidentiality Policy
Governs legal protection and storage of patient records in compliance with UK GDPR and DPA 2018.
2026 🆕
"Broad Consent" Policy (DUAA 2025)
Allows the NHS to use patient data for broad areas of research without needing to re-ask consent for every sub-study.
WEEE · GDPR
Forensic Data Wiping Policy
Mandatory data wiping from all devices during decommissioning to comply with WEEE 2026 and UK GDPR.
Procurement
10% Social Value Policy
Mandatory requirement that at least 10% of any contract score for medical devices is based on Net Zero and community impact.
2026 🆕
Sustainable Procurement Policy
All suppliers must meet Evergreen Level 1 carbon reporting standards by April 2026 to bid for NHS contracts.

Section 04.7

Protocols

Operational Procedures
💡
Interview tip: If asked "How do you ensure a device is safe to return to a ward?" reference Protocol Adherence: follow the local Maintenance Protocol to meet BS EN 62353 benchmarks → Decontamination Protocol (HTM 01-01) → record in CMMS for the CQC Regulation 15 audit trail.
Medical Device Management
Commissioning
Acceptance Testing & Commissioning Protocol
Steps taken when a new device arrives — ensures it meets BS EN 60601-1 standards before it touches a patient.
BS EN 62353
PPM Protocols
Specific technical checklists engineers follow during a service to comply with BS EN 62353.
HTM 01-01
Device Decontamination Protocol
Mandatory steps for cleaning a device before maintenance — includes protein detection checks (below 5 µg/side).
WEEE · GDPR
Asset Disposal & Decommissioning Protocol
Procedures for removing a device from the hospital — forensic data wiping (UK GDPR) and compliant disposal (WEEE 2026).
Clinical Safety & Emergency
Martha's Rule
Rapid Response & Escalation Protocol
Procedure allowing staff or families to trigger an urgent 24/7 Rapid Review Team when a patient deteriorates.
EPRR
Business Continuity & EPRR Protocols
Step-by-step plans for managing medical technology during major failures — power outages and cyber-attacks.
48-hour urgent
NatPSA Response Protocol
Urgent workflow to identify, locate, and remove a device from the clinical area — often within 48 hours — once a National Patient Safety Alert is issued.
Legal
Duty of Candour Protocol
Formal process of meeting with patients and families to provide an apology and full explanation if a device failure has caused harm.
Reporting & Governance
LFPSE · Datix
Incident Reporting Protocol
Workflow for logging an incident or "Good Catch" into the national LFPSE database. Includes Datix for local Trust reporting.
MHRA
Yellow Card Reporting Protocol
Specific steps for reporting medical device malfunctions directly to the MHRA via the Yellow Card system.
KPI · Audit
Audit & KPI Monitoring Protocol
Process for checking the department is meeting its 95% PPM completion target and other performance indicators.
UDI · GL1
"Scan for Safety" Scanning Protocol
Point-of-care procedure where the engineer or clinician scans the device UDI and patient's wristband to ensure full traceability.

Section 04.8

Others — Specialist Topics

Advanced · Interview Power Topics
MDSO Network
Mandatory Role
Medical Device Safety Officer (MDSO)
Mandated role in every NHS Trust. Acts as primary link between the hospital and MHRA. Manages Yellow Card reporting and Field Safety Notices (FSNs). Ensures local device failures are escalated nationally.
Digital Clinical Safety
Software Safety
DCB0129 & DCB0160
DCB0129 — standard for manufacturers to manage clinical risk in health software. DCB0160 — standard for NHS Trusts to manage clinical risk when implementing that software. Essential for any engineer deploying AI tools or integrating devices with EPR systems.
EPRR & Resilience
Emergency
Emergency Preparedness, Resilience & Response (EPRR)
Policy framework for managing national power outages, cyber-attacks, and pandemics. Engineers must maintain Business Continuity Plans for critical life-support equipment and UPS as per HTM 06-01.
COSHH Detail
HSE Enforced
COSHH 2002 — Key Aspects
Legal requirement: assess, manage, and monitor risks from hazardous substances (chemicals, fumes, dust, bacteria). Scope covers chemicals, fumes, dust, vapours, biological agents. Failure risks fines and imprisonment. Part of the broader Health and Safety at Work Act 1974.
Human Factors & Registration
IEC 62366-1
Human Factors & Usability Engineering
In 2026, NHS focus has shifted from technical failure to user error prevention. IEC 62366-1 ensures devices are designed so clinicians don't make mistakes under high-stress conditions. Used during procurement to evaluate device interface safety.
2026 🆕
NMEMS — National Medical Equipment Management System
2026 push to move from local spreadsheets to a nationally linked data hub for device tracking. Mentioning NMEMS in an interview signals awareness of future NHS data infrastructure direction.
RCT · HCPC
Professional Accountability & Registration
Many Trusts now mandate Band 6+ engineers to be on the Register of Clinical Technologists (RCT) or the HCPC. HPOA 2026 introduces statutory duty for Cultural Safety training.
