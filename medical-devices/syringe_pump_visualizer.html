Syringe Interface
Barrel Clamp, Flange Grip & Brand Detection
Syringe Barrel Clamp SAFETY CRITICAL
Spring-loaded clamp holds syringe body firmly against pump chassis. Prevents barrel sliding during infusion — a common siphon failure mode. Check spring tension at PPM; replace if degraded.
Flange Grip / Anti-Pullback Clip SAFETY CRITICAL
Engages rear flange of syringe to prevent barrel being pulled out while plunger is gripped. Without it, negative pressure could aspirate air or cause siphon. Test actuation force and latch integrity.
Plunger Pusher with Release Pads SAFETY CRITICAL
Claw-like mechanism grips plunger head. Release pads must be pressed to disengage — design prevents accidental release mid-infusion. Without plunger grip, negative intrathoracic pressure can aspirate drug (siphon risk).
Barrel Size Sensor ACCURACY
Optical array or linear potentiometer measures syringe barrel diameter. Determines cross-sectional area A to compute flow Q = A·v. Wrong reading = wrong dose. Clean optical path; verify with known syringe at PPM.
Brand Recognition COMPATIBILITY
User selects syringe brand from device menu (BD, Terumo, B.Braun). Each has slightly different internal dimensions. Wrong brand selection = up to 5% accuracy error. Drug library can enforce brand selection.
Force & Pressure
Mechanical Force Translation to Line Pressure
Typical Motor Force Output
50–150N
Determined by stepper torque, gear ratio, lead screw pitch, and efficiency. Too high = patient trauma risk if occluded line releases.
Pressure Per Unit Force (50 mL)
2.5kPa/N
Large bore syringe = lower pressure per unit pusher force. Safer for low-rate delivery but slower occlusion detection.
Pressure Per Unit Force (5 mL)
40kPa/N
Small bore = 16× higher pressure per Newton. Fast occlusion alarm but large bolus stored in tubing compliance. Paediatric concern.
Mechanical Advantage
F = 2πτ·η/p
Lead screw converts rotation to linear force. Small pitch = higher force, slower motion. Typical p = 1 mm/rev.
Electronics & Control
Dual-Processor Closed-Loop System
Control Loop
PID + Encoder + Force
Three feedback paths: encoder for position, current for motor torque, force sensor for plunger resistance. Redundancy catches single-sensor failure.
Safety Architecture
Dual-CPU Independent
Safety CPU can halt motor independently via hardware interrupt, bypassing main CPU. IEC 62304 Class C compliant.
Power Class
Class I + Internal Battery
Mains + Li-ion ≥6 hr (longer than volumetric due to lower mechanical load). CF applied part via isolation.
Software Classification
IEC 62304 Class C
Highest risk category. Each firmware version requires formal validation and regression testing before deployment.
Sensor Array
Detection, Feedback & Interlock Sensors
Pusher Force Sensor (Strain Gauge) SAFETY CRITICAL
Load cell between motor and pusher block. Measures force applied to plunger. Rising force = occlusion. Multi-level thresholds (low/med/high) user-selectable. Most critical sensor for occlusion detection in syringe pumps. Verify calibration with known weights at PPM.
Barrel Size Sensor ACCURACY CRITICAL
Optical emitter/detector array or mechanical linear potentiometer measures syringe outer diameter. Cross-sectional area A used in flow calculation. Wrong reading cascades into wrong dose. Test with reference syringes of known diameter.
Plunger Position Detector INTERLOCK
Confirms plunger is correctly seated in pusher grip. Optical or microswitch-based. Prevents pump run without plunger engagement — critical siphon prevention. Check mechanical actuation and contact bounce.
Flange Clamp Sensor INTERLOCK
Confirms rear flange clip is engaged over syringe flange. Prevents syringe being withdrawn during infusion. Failure mode: user forces syringe with clip not engaged → siphon potential.
Rotary Encoder PERFORMANCE
1000 PPR optical encoder on motor shaft. Validates actual rotation vs. commanded steps. Detects stalled motor, skipped steps, or mechanical binding. Clean encoder disc during PPM.
End-of-Travel Switches LIMITS
Microswitches at full-forward and full-back positions. Prevent mechanical damage from driving beyond range. Trigger "syringe empty" and "syringe loading" states. Test actuation during PPM.
Motor Current Sensor MONITORING
Shunt resistor or Hall effect current sensor in motor driver. Indirect torque measurement — supplements force sensor for redundancy. Elevated current with normal force = mechanical friction buildup.
Temperature Sensor MONITORING
NTC or PT100 on motor/PCB. Protects against overheating — relevant for prolonged high-rate infusions. Also monitors battery cell temperature for safety.

Occlusion Detection
Force Build-Up & Auto Back-Off Feature
Time-to-Alarm (Low Rate)
10–60min
At 1 mL/hr with 50 mL syringe, build-up to alarm can exceed 30 min. Critical risk for ICU vasopressors — patient may be haemodynamically compromised before alarm.
Time-to-Alarm (High Rate)
seconds
At higher flow rates, force builds quickly — rapid detection. Low occlusion threshold reduces TTA but increases nuisance alarms.
Auto Back-Off Feature
Reverse Stepping
After alarm, pump briefly reverses to relieve stored pressure in tubing → reduces post-occlusion bolus delivered when line clears. Not all pumps; verify per model.
Stored Energy Release (Bolus Risk)
E = ½kx²
Syringe plunger compression + tubing compliance store potential energy during occlusion. Released as bolus when line clears. Clinically significant: small-bore syringes store more energy/vol than large.
Siphon Risk
Gravity-Driven Uncontrolled Delivery
Mechanism
Gravity-Driven Flow
If plunger is not securely gripped by pusher and pump is above patient, hydrostatic pressure (P = ρgh) drives uncontrolled drug delivery — potentially several mL in minutes.
Failure Modes
Multiple
Plunger disengaged from pusher; flange clip broken or worn; syringe cracked; loose Luer connection; gravity bag compression acting on partially-loaded syringe.
Critical Risk Scenario
Opioid/Vasopressor
Siphon of potent drug (morphine, noradrenaline, propofol) can deliver lethal bolus in seconds. Death cases documented in MHRA/FDA databases.
Engineering Prevention
4-Layer Defence
(1) Plunger position sensor, (2) flange clip sensor, (3) alarm on disengagement, (4) anti-siphon valve downstream. Verify ALL on every PPM.

Fault Diagnosis
Symptom-Based Fault Trees
⚠ SYMPTOM: Nuisance Occlusion Alarms at Normal Flow
Force sensor offset drift — recalibrate with zero-load reference
Wrong syringe brand selected → higher friction than expected
Lead-screw binding — clean, inspect for wear, re-lubricate
Occlusion threshold set too low for clinical application
Pusher mechanism mis-aligned with plunger axis (lateral friction)
⚠ SYMPTOM: Flow Accuracy Out of Spec (±2%)
Barrel size sensor reading wrong diameter — calibrate with reference syringes
User selected wrong syringe brand in menu
Lead-screw backlash — inspect nut, replace if >0.05 mm play
Encoder disc dirty or misaligned — clean and re-align
Motor step loss at high flow — check driver current setting
⚠ SYMPTOM: "Wrong Syringe" Alarm with Correct Syringe
Barrel sensor optical path contaminated — clean with IPA wipe
Barrel sensor mis-calibrated — verify against reference diameters
Spring tension in barrel clamp degraded — syringe not fully seated
Linear potentiometer worn — replace sensor assembly
⚠ SYMPTOM: Motor Stall / Step Loss Error Code
Lead-screw binding or corrosion — clean, re-lubricate, inspect
Motor driver current set too low — reference service manual
Encoder fault — validate PPR count on service screen
Mechanical obstruction in pusher path (debris, damaged guide)
Excessive load — occluded line + high flow exceeded motor torque
⚠ SYMPTOM: Plunger Disengagement Alarm Intermittent
Plunger sensor contacts worn or dirty — clean/replace switch
Pusher release pads degraded — replace pad assembly
Spring fatigue in pusher grip — replace spring
Wrong syringe brand — plunger head geometry outside grip tolerance
Vibration during patient transport causing momentary disengagement
Accuracy Testing
Gravimetric Trumpet Curves — IEC 60601-2-24
Method
Gravimetric
Analytical balance (±0.001 g). Weigh output over observation window. Volume = mass / density. Use water at room temperature (ρ = 1 g/mL approx).
Standard Requirement
±2%
Steady state error. Excludes startup phase (first 30 min or per IFU) and KVO mode.
Trumpet Curve Windows
2, 5, 11, 19, 31 min
IEC 60601-2-24 requires accuracy at multiple observation windows. Short windows reveal pulsation; long windows reveal mean accuracy.
Startup Bolus (Trumpet Start)
Critical
Mechanical slack take-up before steady flow. Can deliver extra ~0.01–0.1 mL bolus at start. Clinically relevant for potent drugs at low rates.

Key Service Risks
⚠ Siphon: Always test plunger disengagement alarm + flange clip on PPM. Siphon has caused documented deaths.
⚠ Barrel sensor: Wrong brand/size = up to 5% dose error. Verify with reference syringes of each approved brand.
⚠ Force calibration: Force sensor drift = wrong occlusion threshold. Calibrate with reference weight annually.
⚠ Trumpet curve: Low-rate accuracy only visible in long observation windows — single test can miss transient errors.
