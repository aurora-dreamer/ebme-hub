Fluidic Pathway
From IV Bag to Patient
Drip Chamber PASSIVE
Visual flow confirmation + primary air trap. Inspect for cloudiness (contamination), cracks, and partial fill level. Should be ~50% full.
Pump Segment Tubing CONSUMABLE
Single-use PVC segment contacts finger array. Wall thickness tolerance ±0.05 mm critical for accuracy. Tubing ID defines stroke volume per cam cycle.
Anti-Syphon Valve SAFETY
Prevents retrograde flow and free-flow gravity siphon when cassette removed. Test with free-flow test on every PPM. Failure = patient bolus risk.
Downstream Pressure Sensor SAFETY
Piezo strain gauge measures line pressure. Rising pressure = downstream occlusion. Falling pressure = upstream occlusion or empty bag. Calibrate against manometer reference during PPM.
Electronics & Control Architecture
Closed-Loop Delivery System
Control Loop
PID + Encoder Feedback
Encoder PPR → actual speed → PID error correction. Integral windup guard prevents overshoot on flow resumption.
Safety Architecture
Dual-CPU + Watchdog
Independent safety CPU monitors main CPU heartbeat. Failure → immediate clamp actuation. Compliant with IEC 62304 SIL-2.
Power Classes
Class I + Internal Battery
Mains + LiPo battery ≥4 hr autonomy. Leakage current <10 µA (CF applied part). Earth continuity tested at PPM.
Software Class
IEC 62304 Class C
Highest risk class. All firmware updates require validation. Document version with device asset record.
Sensor Array
Detection, Feedback & Interlock Sensors
Ultrasonic Air-in-Line Detector SAFETY CRITICAL
Piezoelectric transceiver pair across tubing. Air attenuates ultrasound signal. Threshold typically 50 µL bubble. Test with air injection during PPM. Contamination with fluid on ultrasonic face causes false negatives.
Downstream Occlusion Pressure Sensor SAFETY CRITICAL
Piezoresistive strain gauge. Alarm threshold typically 300–900 mmHg (user configurable). Verify calibration with dead-weight tester or reference manometer. Check O-ring seals for fluid ingress.
Door / Cassette Presence Switch INTERLOCK
Microswitch or Hall effect sensor. Prevents pump run with door open. Verify actuation force and contact bounce during PPM. Worn actuator = intermittent alarm without true door fault.
Rotary Encoder (Motor Feedback) PERFORMANCE
1000 PPR optical encoder on motor shaft. Validates actual vs commanded step count. Discrepancy triggers motor fault. Clean encoder disc from contamination; check phototransistor alignment.
Upstream / Empty Bag Sensor ADVISORY
Either pressure-drop method (vacuum forms upstream) or dedicated optical drop detector on drip chamber. False empty-bag alarms: damaged drop detector, too-low drip chamber fill, very viscous fluids.
Temperature Sensor (NTC/PT100) MONITORING
Motor and PCB thermal monitoring. Overtemperature shuts pump to prevent fluid warming. Relevant for neonatal and ICU warming applications.

Occlusion Detection
Pressure-Time Signature Analysis
Time-to-Alarm (TTA)
Rate-Dependent
At 1 mL/hr, TTA can exceed 30 min. At 500 mL/hr, seconds. Clinically critical for low-rate vasopressors — patient may receive a bolus when occlusion clears.
Stored Energy (Bolus Risk)
E = ½kx²
Compliant tubing stores elastic energy during occlusion. On clearance → bolus delivery. Stiffer tubing = less stored energy = safer, but higher TTA.

Fault Diagnosis
Systematic Fault Tree
⚠ SYMPTOM: Persistent Occlusion Alarm — No Obvious Blockage
Pressure sensor drift or offset — recalibrate against reference manometer
Tubing kinked inside cassette door — inspect routing; replace tubing
Alarm threshold set too low by user — review clinical settings
O-ring on pressure port degraded — replace, check for fluid ingress into sensor
⚠ SYMPTOM: Inaccurate Flow Rate (gravimetric failure >±5%)
Worn cam mechanism — inspect cam profile for flat spots; replace drive assembly
Non-compliant tubing (wrong brand/lot) — verify tubing compatibility with pump model
Encoder signal degraded — clean optical encoder disc; verify PPR count
Backlash in gearbox — test for motor steps without cam movement
⚠ SYMPTOM: Nuisance Air-in-Line Alarms
Ultrasonic face contaminated with condensation or fluid — dry carefully with IPA wipe
Tubing not fully seated against ultrasonic sensor face
Microbubbles from cold IV bag warming — prime tubing fully before connecting
Transducer coupling degraded — replacement of air-detector assembly
⚠ SYMPTOM: Battery Not Holding Charge
LiPo cell aging — capacity test; replace if <80% rated Ah at 2C discharge
BMS (Battery Management System) fault — error code on service menu
Charge circuit PCB failure — measure charge voltage at battery terminals during mains operation
Accuracy Testing & Verification
Gravimetric Method — IEC 60601-2-24 Compliant
Method
Gravimetric
Analytical balance (±0.001 g resolution). Water density = 1 g/mL. Weigh output over defined period. Volume = mass ÷ density.
Standard Requirement
±2%steady state
IEC 60601-2-24 §201.12.1. Excludes first 30 min warm-up, KVO mode, and bolus delivery phase.
Test Rates
3 Points Minimum
Low (5 mL/hr), mid (100 mL/hr), high (500 mL/hr). Each measured ≥30 min after startup. Repeat ×3 and average.
VTBI Accuracy
±5%of set volume
Total volume delivered vs. programmed. Important for chemotherapy and paediatric applications. Log in device record.

Clinical Risk Notes
⚠ Free-flow: Always test anti-syphon on PPM. Door-open free-flow is a Never Event category risk.
⚠ Occlusion bolus: Document occ. pressure threshold changes in device record. ICU vasopressors most at risk.
⚠ Drug library: Verify library version matches pharmacy formulary. DERS bypass events are reportable under MHRA Yellow Card.
⚠ Tubing: Only approved tubing per IFU. Non-compliant sets invalidate accuracy spec.
