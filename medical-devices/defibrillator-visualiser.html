Rhythm Analysis, Charge Control & Shock Advisory
Shock Advisory (AED mode)
Rhythm Classification Algorithm
Detects VF/pulseless VT vs. non-shockable rhythms. Validated against ANSI/AAMI DF80 rhythm library — never modify without manufacturer authorisation.
Safety Architecture
Independent HV Enable Path
Physical shock button/trigger and HV enable are gated separately from the main CPU — prevents single-fault accidental discharge.
Self-Test Regime
Automated Daily/Weekly
Verifies battery, capacitor charge/discharge path, and internal circuitry without full energy delivery. Logged event history reviewed at PPM.
Software Class
IEC 62304 Class C
Highest risk classification — rhythm analysis and energy delivery logic. Firmware version recorded in asset record.
Sensor Array
Contact, Feedback & Interlock Sensors
Pad/Paddle Contact Sensing SAFETY CRITICAL
Impedance measurement confirms adequate skin contact before charge/shock is permitted. Poor gel coverage or dried gel pads give false "check electrode" warnings — a leading nuisance-fault cause.
HV Divider / Feedback Sensor SAFETY CRITICAL
Measures capacitor voltage for closed-loop charge control and confirms actual delivered energy for the event log. Verify calibration against defibrillator analyzer at PPM.
Current-Sense Transformer SAFETY CRITICAL
In-circuit during discharge; feeds real-time impedance compensation. Degraded sensing causes energy delivery outside tolerance across the impedance range.
ECG/Rhythm Input (via pads or leads) MONITORING
Defib-proof front end, isolated to survive the HV discharge pulse without damage. Verify recovery time and rhythm display accuracy with a cardiac simulator.
Paddle/Pad Presence & Cable Continuity INTERLOCK
Detects disconnected or damaged pad cable — prevents charge/shock attempt with an open circuit. Inspect cable strain-relief points during PPM; a common failure site.
CPR Feedback Accelerometer (where fitted) ADVISORY
Measures compression rate/depth for real-time CPR quality feedback. Verify against manufacturer test rig if fitted.

Safety Interlocks
Preventing Unintended or Unsafe Energy Delivery
Charge Timeout Auto-Discharge SAFETY CRITICAL
If no shock delivered within the manufacturer-set window (typically 60 s, or immediately on energy re-selection), the dump resistor safely bleeds the capacitor. Verify function every PPM — a failed dump path leaves stored HV energy live.
Pad Impedance Gating SAFETY CRITICAL
Charge/shock is blocked outside the validated impedance window (typically 25–200 Ω), preventing ineffective or hazardous delivery on a poor contact.
Physical Shock Button Isolation SAFETY CRITICAL
Manual mode requires deliberate two-handed or dual-action activation, physically and electronically separate from charge control, reducing accidental discharge risk.
Sync Mode R-Wave Gate CLINICAL SAFETY
In cardioversion mode, discharge is withheld until a valid R-wave is detected — prevents delivery during the vulnerable T-wave period which can induce VF.

Fault Diagnosis
Systematic Fault Tree
⚠ SYMPTOM: Excessive Charge Time / Charge Fails to Complete
Storage capacitor degraded (increased ESR with age) — verify against defibrillator analyzer charge-time spec
Battery depleted or degraded — run capacity/load test; replace if below manufacturer threshold
DC-DC inverter or HV transformer fault — check charge waveform on service mode/oscilloscope
Charge feedback divider drifted — recalibrate HV sense path
⚠ SYMPTOM: Energy Delivered Outside Tolerance
Current-sense transformer fault — impedance compensation not functioning correctly
H-bridge IGBT degraded/partially failed — capture waveform, compare tilt/duration to reference
HV capacitor capacitance drift — measure against manufacturer spec; replace if out of tolerance
Test performed at only one impedance — repeat across full 25–200 Ω range per IEC 60601-2-4
⚠ SYMPTOM: Persistent "Check Electrodes" / Poor Contact Warning
Expired or dried pad gel — check expiry date; pads are single-use consumables
Pad cable connector damaged/corroded — inspect and clean connector pins
Internal impedance-sense circuit fault — verify with impedance simulator across range
⚠ SYMPTOM: Self-Test Failure at Power-On
Battery below self-test threshold — check charge state before assuming hardware fault
Internal RTC/clock battery depleted — some units flag this as a self-test fault
Review event log for specific fault code before escalating — avoid blind component replacement
Accuracy Testing & Verification
Defibrillator Analyzer Method — IEC 60601-2-4 Compliant
Method
Defibrillator Analyzer
Dedicated test load (e.g. Fluke QA-56, Datrend DefibsPro) measures delivered energy, waveform, and charge time across a range of simulated patient impedances.
Energy Accuracy
±15%or ±3 J
Whichever is greater, verified at low, mid, and maximum energy settings.
Test Impedances
Minimum 3 Points
Typically 25 Ω, 50 Ω, 100 Ω, 150–200 Ω to verify impedance compensation across the clinical range.
Waveform Verification
Capture & Compare
Pulse duration, tilt, and phase reversal timing checked against manufacturer reference waveform.

Clinical Risk Notes
⚠ Residual HV energy: Never assume a capacitor is discharged — verify auto-discharge function and follow lockout/tagout before internal work.
⚠ Pad expiry: Expired pads increase impedance and contact failure risk — treat as a consumable stock-control item, not just a clinical one.
⚠ Sync mode: Verify sync marker aligns correctly with R-wave; a missed or mistimed sync shock is a reportable clinical incident.
⚠ Self-test logs: A device that "passed" self-test but shows repeated transient faults in its log should be investigated, not just reset.
